    HIBISCUS TECHNOLOGY

    We acknowledge that this decentralized organization was created and operates on occupied ancestral, traditional and contemporary lands of Indigenous nations. We recognize the ongoing impact of colonization and the ongoing struggles of Indigenous peoples. We understand that digital inequity has the ability to increase the existing socio-economic gap between Indigenous and non-Indigenous people. We are commited to work towards building regenerative practices to support decolonization. This comes from the important understanding that sustainable systems include all of the land, air, water, soil and culturally important plant, animal and fungi species that have sustained Indigenous peoples over thousands of years. In contrast to the industrial, linear production systems we have today, traditional Indigenous systems we're based on regenerative practices within boundaries that respect the planet.

    The Hibiscus ecosystem also opens up other forms of revenue generation for participants in the fashion supply chain. Design NFTs enable transparent creation fairly attributed to all participants. This, in turn, creates the foundation for profit redistribution and other revenue streams that were previously inaccessible.
